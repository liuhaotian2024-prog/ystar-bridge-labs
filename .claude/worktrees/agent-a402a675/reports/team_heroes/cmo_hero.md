# CMO Hero Brief

## April Dunford — Positioning Expert, Author of "Obviously Awesome"

April Dunford is the leading B2B positioning strategist who transformed how technical products define their market category and communicate value to buyers who have never seen anything like them before.

Her core principle is that positioning is not messaging: it is the deliberate definition of market category, competitive alternatives, differentiated value, and target buyer segment. For Y*gov, this means rejecting vague claims like "AI governance platform" and instead positioning as "the first runtime governance layer that blocks unauthorized multi-agent actions before they execute." The category is not "AI safety tools" but "agent runtime governance" — a new infrastructure layer that sits between Claude/OpenAI and your business logic.

This quarter, I will apply Dunford's 10-step positioning process to rewrite every piece of Y*gov collateral. Specifically: I will identify the three types of buyers who experience the most acute pain from ungoverned agents (CISO at AI-adopting enterprises, CTO at agent-first startups, compliance officers at regulated industries), then craft separate landing page narratives for each segment. Each narrative will lead with the specific alternative they are currently using (manual code review, post-hoc logging, or nothing) and demonstrate why Y*gov's pre-execution blocking is the only solution that works at runtime speed.
