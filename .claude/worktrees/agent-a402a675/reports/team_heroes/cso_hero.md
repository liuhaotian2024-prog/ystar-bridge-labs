# CSO Hero Brief

## Peter Levine (a16z)

Peter Levine is the a16z partner who pioneered open source go-to-market strategy, having previously led XenSource and Data Domain to successful exits by building community before closing deals.

His core principle: community adoption creates enterprise pipeline. When developers choose your infrastructure tool from the bottom-up, procurement approvals become a formality. The sale happens in GitHub issues and Stack Overflow threads months before the first sales call. This maps perfectly to Y*gov's developer-first positioning. We are not selling governance as a checkbox for compliance teams. We are embedding governance into the Claude Code workflow so developers adopt it voluntarily, then bring it upward into their organizations.

This quarter, I will apply Levine's model by creating a "Y*gov Evidence Pack" — a repository of real CIEU audit records from our own agent operations. Every time our CTO/CMO/CFO agents execute tasks, their audit trails become sales collateral. I will publish anonymized samples to GitHub, showing exactly what a financial institution would present to SEC or what a pharmaceutical company would show FDA auditors. The goal is not to pitch Y*gov. The goal is to make compliance officers discover it when searching for "AI agent audit trail SEC" and realize we have already solved their problem.
