# CASE-006: HN and LinkedIn Posts Exceeded Platform Length Norms

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Agent:** CMO
**Severity:** Medium

## What Was the Task
CMO prepared Series 1 (HN) and LinkedIn founding article for Board to publish.

## What Decision Was Made
CMO wrote both posts at essay length without researching platform-specific norms. HN post and LinkedIn article both exceeded optimal word counts, forcing the chairman to manually edit and trim before publishing.

## What Framework Was Applied
None. CMO wrote content without first researching platform character limits, optimal engagement lengths, or top-performing post patterns on each platform.

## What Was the Outcome
Chairman had to manually rewrite/trim both posts under time pressure (8:30 ET deadline for HN). This is unacceptable — the chairman's time should never be spent on work that agents should have done correctly.

## What to Do Differently
1. BEFORE writing for ANY platform, research that platform's specific constraints:
   - Character/word limits
   - Optimal length for engagement
   - Style and tone preferences
   - Top-performing post patterns
2. Delegate research tasks to Jinjin (cheaper, per Jinjin Delegation Protocol)
3. Study top-tier examples on each platform before drafting
4. First draft must already comply with platform constraints — not "write long, cut later"

## Root Cause
Same pattern as CASE-001/002: agent produced output optimized for looking thorough rather than being fit for purpose. Writing long feels like doing more work. Platform-aware brevity requires more skill, not less.

## Y*gov Product Insight
Content quality is a semantic-layer problem. Y*gov can enforce word count limits via invariant checks, but "good writing" is beyond deterministic enforcement. This requires knowledge-layer discipline (research before write) not just contract-layer rules.
