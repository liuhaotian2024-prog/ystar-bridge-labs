## Distilled Strategic Positions

### Position 1 — Reject the speed-race
- We do NOT compete on 30-min hackathon / $50-trivial-task markets
- vibe-coder competitors will always win on raw reaction speed (no governance overhead)
- Loss-leader markets are NOT loss-leaders for us; they're structural losses

### Position 2 — Own the trust-race
- Markets where regression-sensitivity, audit-trail, multi-file refactors, compliance/finance/security domains dominate
- Governance overhead is **setup cost, not per-task cost** — amortizes over task complexity and duration
- $5000+ multi-day bounties: our CIEU trace + DNA_LOG audit + obligation tracking IS the deliverable's quality guarantee

### Position 3 — Build-phase sunk costs are non-negotiable
- We've already paid the governance setup cost (CIEU schema, CZL contracts, enforce_roster, hook daemon)
- Reversing now to chase speed = throwing away sunk investment AND losing the moat
- Forward path: lean into the moat, don't apologize for it

### Position 4 — Secondary monetization: governance-as-data
- CIEU 6503+ events is potentially the **first real multi-agent governance trace dataset** in academia/industry
- Sell access to the trace data → academic licensing / industry benchmarks / training-data for governance research
- This is a Y* Bridge Labs-unique asset; nobody else has months of dogfooded multi-agent governance trace at this density

## Analogy

**Law firm vs. street-corner letter-writer**:
- Street letter-writer: faster, cheaper for trivial requests (rental-deposit complaint)
- Law firm: slower setup, billable-hour overhead, but DOMINATES anything requiring liability, regulatory compliance, or cross-jurisdiction reasoning
- Y* Bridge Labs = law firm of the agent-execution market. Pick markets where we can dominate, not where we're forced to compete on raw speed.
