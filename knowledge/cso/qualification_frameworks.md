# Sales Qualification Frameworks — CSO Knowledge Base

## MEDDIC (Primary Framework for Y*gov)

**Definition**: Sales qualification checklist ensuring you pursue winnable, valuable deals. MEDDIC = Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion.

**When to use**: Every enterprise opportunity >$25K ARR. Required before spending significant time on proposal or demo.

**Step-by-step**:

**M = Metrics** (Quantifiable impact)
- Question: "What's the business impact of this problem?"
- For Y*gov: "How much time does your compliance team spend documenting AI agent actions?" or "What's the cost if you fail an SEC audit?"
- Document specific numbers: "$400K in audit remediation" or "80 hours/month in manual documentation"
- Red flag: Prospect can't quantify pain ("It's just inefficient")

**E = Economic Buyer** (Budget authority)
- Question: "Who has authority to allocate budget for this?"
- Not the same as your champion. Title usually: CFO, CTO, Chief Compliance Officer, VP Procurement.
- Test: "If we solve [pain] for [price], who signs the contract?"
- Red flag: "I need to ask my boss" (you're talking to wrong person)

**D = Decision Criteria** (How they'll evaluate)
- Question: "What are your top 3-5 requirements for selecting a governance solution?"
- Write down their exact words. Use these in proposal.
- For Y*gov typical answers: "SEC audit readiness", "No performance impact", "Easy developer adoption"
- Red flag: Vague criteria ("We need something robust")

**D = Decision Process** (Steps to close)
- Question: "Walk me through what happens after this call."
- Map out: Technical evaluation → Legal review → Security approval → Procurement → Signature
- Get timeline: "Security review takes 2 weeks, procurement takes 1 week"
- Red flag: "We'll figure it out" (no defined process = dead deal)

**I = Identify Pain** (Current broken workflow)
- Question: "What happens today when [scenario]?"
- For Y*gov: "What do you show an auditor when they ask what your AI agent accessed?"
- Look for emotional language: "It's a nightmare", "We're terrified", "Cost us a fortune"
- Red flag: "It's not ideal but manageable" (pain isn't urgent)

**C = Champion** (Internal advocate)
- Question: "Would you present Y*gov to your leadership on our behalf?"
- Champion sells when you're not in the room. Gives you insider info.
- Test: Ask for introduction to economic buyer. If they refuse, they're not a champion.
- Red flag: "I'm interested but don't have time to help" (not a real champion)

**Common mistakes**:
- Checking boxes without depth (asking questions but not documenting answers)
- Confusing interest with qualification (they like product ≠ they'll buy)
- Not revisiting MEDDIC after each call (situations change)
- Moving to proposal with incomplete MEDDIC (wastes time on unwinnable deals)

**Scoring**:
- 6/6 MEDDIC elements = Qualified Opportunity (invest time)
- 4-5/6 = Needs development (schedule follow-up to fill gaps)
- <4/6 = Disqualify or deprioritize

## BANT (Legacy Framework — Use for Quick Filtering)

**Definition**: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline. Simple qualification for initial filtering.

**When to use**: First call with cold lead. BANT tells you if it's worth a deeper MEDDIC qualification.

**Step-by-step**:

**B = Budget**
- Question: "What budget have you allocated for governance/compliance tools this year?"
- Or: "What's your typical spend on security/compliance software?"
- Red flag: "No budget" (unless pain is urgent enough to create budget)

**A = Authority**
- Question: "Are you the decision-maker for this purchase?"
- If no: "Who else is involved?"
- Red flag: "I'll need to ask several people" (deal will be slow)

**N = Need**
- Question: "What's driving this search right now?"
- Looking for urgency: regulatory deadline, recent audit failure, incident.
- Red flag: "Just exploring options" (tire-kicker)

**T = Timeline**
- Question: "When do you need this implemented?"
- Specific date > vague timeframe ("Q2" is better than "sometime this year")
- Red flag: "No rush" (will never close)

**BANT Score**:
- 4/4 = Pass to MEDDIC qualification
- 2-3/4 = Nurture (add to email drip campaign, revisit quarterly)
- 0-1/4 = Disqualify

**Common mistakes**:
- Using BANT as only qualification (too shallow for complex sales)
- Accepting "we have budget" without amount (might be $5K when you need $50K)
- Not asking about timeline (leads to perpetual "thinking about it")

## SPICED (Modern Alternative to BANT)

**Definition**: Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision. More customer-centric than BANT.

**When to use**: Discovery calls with leads who contacted you (inbound). Better than BANT for consultative selling.

**Step-by-step**:

**S = Situation**
- Question: "Tell me about your current AI agent compliance workflow."
- Goal: Understand context. How many agents? What do they do? Who's responsible?
- Document their tech stack, team structure, current tools.

**P = Pain**
- Question: "What's not working about your current approach?"
- Let them talk. Use silence. They'll reveal real pain.
- For Y*gov: "We have no audit trail", "Compliance team doesn't trust AI outputs"

**I = Impact**
- Question: "What happens if this doesn't get solved?"
- Looking for business impact: regulatory risk, customer trust, operational cost.
- Quantify: "If you can't prove AI agent actions, what's the SEC fine exposure?"

**C = Critical Event**
- Question: "Why now? Why not 6 months ago?"
- Triggers: upcoming audit, new regulation, recent incident, new leadership mandate.
- Red flag: No critical event = low urgency = long sales cycle

**D = Decision**
- Question: "What's your decision process and timeline?"
- Same as MEDDIC's Decision Process.
- Who evaluates, who approves, what are the steps, how long?

**Common mistakes**:
- Rushing through Situation (context is critical for tailoring pitch)
- Not quantifying Impact (vague pain = no urgency)
- Accepting "we should do this" without Critical Event (will deprioritize you)

**When to use SPICED vs MEDDIC**:
- SPICED: Inbound leads, early discovery, consultative approach
- MEDDIC: Enterprise deals, formal evaluation, multiple stakeholders

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Definition

**Definition**: Detailed description of companies that get most value from Y*gov, close fastest, have highest LTV, lowest churn.

**When to use**: Before any prospecting. ICP guides who you target.

**Y*gov ICP** (defined):

**Type A: Financial Services Compliance**
- Company size: 500-5000 employees (enterprise compliance teams but not too bureaucratic)
- Industry: Banks, hedge funds, FinTech, asset managers
- Trigger: SEC/FINRA increasing scrutiny on AI, recent audit, new AI initiatives
- Pain: No audit trail for AI agent decisions in regulated workflows
- Decision maker: Chief Compliance Officer, Head of Regulatory Affairs
- Budget: $100K-$500K annual compliance software spend
- Sales cycle: 3-6 months (legal review heavy)

**Type B: Pharmaceutical/Healthcare**
- Company size: 1000-10000 employees
- Industry: Big Pharma, biotech, CRO (Clinical Research Organizations)
- Trigger: FDA validation requirements, new AI tools in drug development
- Pain: FDA requires complete records of all automated operations
- Decision maker: VP IT, Head of Quality Assurance, Validation Lead
- Budget: $200K-$1M (validation budgets are large)
- Sales cycle: 6-12 months (validation process is slow)

**Type C: Developer Tools Companies**
- Company size: 50-500 employees
- Industry: SaaS companies building with AI agents, AI startups
- Trigger: Moving AI agents from prototype to production, customer audit requests
- Pain: Managing multi-agent workflows, permission inheritance
- Decision maker: CTO, VP Engineering
- Budget: $10K-$100K
- Sales cycle: 1-3 months (developers decide fast)

**Anti-ICP** (who NOT to pursue):
- Government agencies (procurement is 12+ months)
- Companies <50 employees (no compliance budget)
- Non-AI companies (no pain yet)
- Free trial seekers with no budget

**Common mistakes**:
- Targeting everyone ("any company with AI agents")
- Not updating ICP based on win/loss analysis (quarterly review required)
- Sales team doesn't know ICP (must train CSO/SDRs on exact profile)

## Lead Scoring Model

**Definition**: Point system to prioritize which leads get CSO attention first.

**When to use**: When you have more leads than CSO can handle. Automate scoring in CRM.

**Y*gov Lead Scoring** (100 point scale):

**Firmographic (30 points)**:
- Company in ICP industry: +20 (finance, pharma, AI-forward tech)
- Company size 500-5000 employees: +10
- Outside ICP: -10

**Behavioral (40 points)**:
- Installed Y*gov: +20
- Generated 10+ audits: +15
- 3+ employees from same company using Y*gov: +25
- Attended webinar/demo: +10
- Downloaded compliance whitepaper: +5

**BANT Qualification (30 points)**:
- Has budget: +10
- Decision maker: +10
- Urgent timeline (<3 months): +10

**Lead Priority Tiers**:
- 80-100 points: Hot (CSO immediate outreach)
- 60-79 points: Warm (CSO outreach within 1 week)
- 40-59 points: Nurture (automated email sequence)
- <40 points: Disqualify or long-term nurture

**Common mistakes**:
- Scoring activity over intent (100 email opens ≠ buying signal)
- Not decaying scores over time (lead from 6 months ago shouldn't be "hot")
- Sales team ignores scores (must enforce: only work leads >60 points)

## Disqualification Criteria (When to Walk Away)

**Definition**: Hard rules for when to stop pursuing a deal. Protecting CSO time.

**When to use**: After qualification call. Better to walk away early than waste months.

**Disqualify if**:

1. No quantifiable pain
   - Test: "What's the cost of not solving this?"
   - If answer is vague, they won't pay for solution.

2. No budget, no willingness to create budget
   - Test: "If we solve this for $X, can you allocate budget?"
   - If "no" or "maybe next year," deprioritize.

3. No access to economic buyer
   - Test: "Can you introduce me to [decision maker]?"
   - If they refuse or stall repeatedly, you're stuck.

4. Timeline >12 months
   - Exception: If it's normal for their industry (pharma validation).
   - Otherwise: add to long-term nurture, don't actively work.

5. Decision criteria you can't meet
   - Example: "Must be on-premise only" (if Y*gov is cloud-only)
   - Don't waste time trying to change requirements.

6. Champion won't advocate
   - Test: "Will you present this to your team?"
   - If "I don't have time" or "Just send me materials," not a champion.

7. Current vendor has 3+ year contract
   - Ask: "When does your current contract renew?"
   - If they're locked in, revisit 6 months before renewal.

8. They want free consulting, not a product
   - Sign: Asking for custom architecture reviews, detailed compliance guides, but no buying intent.
   - Response: "Happy to do that as part of a pilot. Should we discuss terms?"

**How to disqualify gracefully**:
- "Based on what you've shared, it sounds like timing isn't right. Can I check back in [6 months/Q4/next year]?"
- Add to nurture campaign. Don't burn bridges.

**Common mistakes**:
- Not disqualifying (CSO wastes time on dead deals)
- Disqualifying too quickly (missing deals that need development)
- Not documenting disqualification reason (can't learn from patterns)

## Qualification Checklist (Use on Every Call)

After discovery call, answer:
- [ ] Can they quantify the pain in dollars or hours?
- [ ] Have I identified the economic buyer by name and title?
- [ ] Do I know their decision criteria and process?
- [ ] Is there a critical event driving urgency?
- [ ] Do I have a champion who will advocate internally?
- [ ] Is timeline <6 months (or normal for industry)?
- [ ] Does this match our ICP?

If 5+ boxes checked: Qualified (move to proposal)
If 3-4 boxes checked: Develop (schedule follow-up)
If <3 boxes checked: Disqualify or long-term nurture

## Framework Selection Guide

**Use MEDDIC when**: Enterprise deal, >$50K ARR, multiple stakeholders, formal procurement
**Use BANT when**: Initial cold call, quick filtering, transactional sale
**Use SPICED when**: Inbound lead, consultative sale, need to understand context deeply
**Use ICP when**: Prospecting (who to target)
**Use Lead Scoring when**: Too many leads, need to prioritize CSO time
**Use Disqualification Criteria when**: Gut says deal is stuck, need objective decision to walk away

Different frameworks for different stages. BANT → SPICED → MEDDIC is a common progression.
