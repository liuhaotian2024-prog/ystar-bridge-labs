### Title: "Who Governs the Agents?"

**What we're doing:**
We're building a real company where AI agents do the work and govern themselves using the product we sell. Every decision is audited, tamper-proof, and public on GitHub. Day 11. 806 tests. 38 governance tools. Zero fabrication tolerance.

**Why this matters:**
AI agents are reading your files, running your commands, and making decisions with real consequences. The question isn't whether to let them — it's who makes sure they do it right. Not after the fact. Not with another AI watching. With deterministic, mathematically verifiable rules that no prompt can bypass. We believe this is a civilizational problem, and we're solving it by eating our own dogfood — running our entire company on the governance system we built.

**What we've proven:**
50 attack variants, 100% blocked. 50 concurrent agents, zero data leaks. 16 internal governance mechanisms verified live — not mocked, not simulated, actually running. Pearl Level 2 causal inference producing real confidence scores. And the first documented case of one AI governance system governing agents powered by a different AI model, across different hardware.

Developers who've had an AI agent do something it shouldn't. Security researchers who think about runtime enforcement. Anyone who believes transparency isn't just a value — it's an engineering requirement.
